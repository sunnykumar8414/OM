Date 1999
            In 1999, the United Nations faced a pivotal 
            moment as it prepared for the new 
            millennium. While there wasn't a single
            "convocation" by that name, the 1999
            Commission on Sustainable Development (CSD-7)
            served as the critical global gathering
            focused on the burgeoning crisis of 
            sanitation and the protection of the world's 
            oceans {ref 1}.

            During this period, the UN identified that 
            over 2.4 billion people lacked basic 
            sanitation, a "global natural problem" that 
            bridged the gap between public health and 
            environmental integrity. The discussions 
            centered on the fact that human waste was 
            not just a medical issue, but a primary 
            pollutant of the global commons-the 
            oceans. The 1999 sessions emphasized 
            that "cleanliness" was a prerequisite for 
            sustainable development, leading to the
